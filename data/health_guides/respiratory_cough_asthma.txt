RESPIRATORY CONDITIONS — COUGH, ASTHMA, BREATHING PROBLEMS

Common Respiratory Symptoms:
- Cough (khansi) — dry or with phlegm
- Wheezing — whistling sound while breathing
- Shortness of breath (saans phoolna)
- Chest tightness
- Sore throat (gale me kharaash)
- Nasal congestion (nak band)

Acute Cough Causes:
- Viral upper respiratory infection (common cold)
- Bronchitis
- Allergies and dust exposure
- Air pollution (common in urban India)
- Post-nasal drip
- Acid reflux (GERD)

Asthma Symptoms:
- Recurring wheezing
- Shortness of breath especially at night
- Chest tightness
- Cough worse at night or early morning
- Symptoms triggered by dust, pollen, cold air, exercise, smoke

Pneumonia Warning Signs:
- High fever with cough
- Rust-colored or bloody sputum
- Sharp chest pain while breathing
- Rapid breathing
- Confusion (especially in elderly)
- Requires prompt medical attention

TB (Tuberculosis) Signs:
- Persistent cough for more than 3 weeks
- Blood in sputum
- Night sweats
- Weight loss
- Low-grade fever
- Must be evaluated by doctor immediately

Self-Care for Mild Respiratory Symptoms:
- Stay hydrated — warm water, herbal tea with ginger and honey
- Steam inhalation 2-3 times daily for congestion
- Gargle with warm salt water for throat pain
- Use humidifier in dry environments
- Avoid smoke, dust, and strong perfumes
- Rest and avoid strenuous activity
- Honey and ginger for cough relief
- Elevate head while sleeping for post-nasal drip
- Avoid cold beverages if throat is irritated

For Asthma Management:
- Identify and avoid triggers
- Keep rescue inhaler accessible if prescribed
- Never stop prescribed inhaler without doctor advice
- Monitor peak flow if advised by doctor
- Get flu vaccination annually

When to Seek Emergency Care:
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Lips or fingernails turning blue
- Cannot speak in full sentences due to breathlessness
- Chest pain with breathing difficulty
- Coughing blood
- High fever with severe cough

Prevention:
- Avoid smoking and secondhand smoke
- Wear mask in high pollution areas
- Keep living space dust-free
- Vaccinate against flu and pneumonia (if eligible)
- Practice good hand hygiene

Note: Persistent cough beyond 3 weeks needs medical evaluation. Educational information only.
