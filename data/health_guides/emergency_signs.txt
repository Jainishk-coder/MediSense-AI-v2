EMERGENCY HEALTH SIGNS — WHEN TO SEEK IMMEDIATE CARE

Life-Threatening Emergencies — Call Ambulance (102/108 in India) Immediately:

Heart Emergency:
- Severe chest pain or pressure
- Pain spreading to arm, jaw, or back
- Sudden shortness of breath
- Irregular or very fast heartbeat with dizziness

Stroke Signs (FAST):
- Face drooping on one side
- Arm weakness — cannot raise both arms
- Speech difficulty — slurred or confused speech
- Time to call emergency immediately
- Every minute counts in stroke treatment

Breathing Emergency:
- Cannot breathe or severe breathlessness
- Lips or face turning blue
- Choking
- Severe asthma attack not responding to inhaler

Other Emergencies:
- Unconsciousness or not responding
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Seizures lasting more than 5 minutes
- Severe allergic reaction — swelling of face/throat, difficulty breathing
- High fever with stiff neck and confusion (meningitis sign)
- Severe head injury with vomiting or confusion
- Suspected poisoning
- Snake bite with swelling
- Severe burns covering large area
- Suicidal thoughts — call mental health helpline (Kiran: 1800-599-0019)

High Fever Emergency:
- Fever above 104°F (40°C) in adults
- Fever with rash that doesn't fade on pressure
- Fever with severe headache and neck stiffness
- Fever in newborn (under 3 months) — any fever is emergency

Pregnancy Emergencies:
- Severe abdominal pain
- Heavy vaginal bleeding
- Severe headache with vision changes
- Reduced fetal movement
- Water breaking before due date

What to Do in Emergency:
1. Call ambulance or go to nearest hospital ER
2. Do not drive yourself if having chest pain or stroke symptoms
3. Note time when symptoms started
4. Keep patient calm and lying down if possible
5. Do not give food or water if vomiting or unconscious
6. Carry list of medications patient is taking

Important Helplines in India:
- National Ambulance: 102 / 108
- Medical Emergency: 112
- Mental Health (Kiran): 1800-599-0019
- Poison Control: 1066 (check local number)

Note: This guide is for awareness only. In any emergency, seek professional medical help immediately.
