DENGUE, MALARIA AND TYPHOID — HEALTH GUIDE

DENGUE FEVER

What is Dengue?
A viral infection spread by Aedes mosquito bites. Common during monsoon season in India.

Symptoms (Lakshan):
- Sudden high fever (104°F / 40°C)
- Severe headache (tez sir dard)
- Pain behind the eyes
- Joint and muscle pain (breakbone fever)
- Skin rash — appears 3-4 days after fever
- Nausea and vomiting
- Mild bleeding — nose or gums

Warning Signs (Dengue Hemorrhagic — EMERGENCY):
- Severe abdominal pain
- Persistent vomiting
- Bleeding from nose or gums
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Extreme fatigue and restlessness
- Cold, clammy skin
- Seek IMMEDIATE hospital care

Self-Care:
- Rest completely
- Drink plenty of fluids — ORS, coconut water, juice
- Paracetamol for fever (AVOID aspirin and ibuprofen — bleeding risk)
- Monitor platelet count if advised by doctor
- Use mosquito net and repellent
- Sponge with lukewarm water for fever

Prevention:
- Remove stagnant water around home
- Use mosquito repellent and nets
- Wear full-sleeve clothes during monsoon
- Keep windows screened

MALARIA

What is Malaria?
Parasitic infection spread by Anopheles mosquito bites.

Symptoms:
- High fever with chills and sweating (cycles every 48-72 hours)
- Headache
- Body ache
- Nausea and vomiting
- Weakness
- Enlarged spleen in chronic cases

Self-Care (while awaiting treatment):
- Rest and hydration
- Complete the full course of antimalarial medication as prescribed
- Never self-medicate — wrong drug can be dangerous

Prevention:
- Sleep under insecticide-treated mosquito net
- Use insect repellent
- Antimalarial prophylaxis when traveling to endemic areas

TYPHOID FEVER

What is Typhoid?
Bacterial infection (Salmonella typhi) from contaminated food or water.

Symptoms:
- Prolonged high fever (step-ladder pattern)
- Weakness and fatigue
- Headache
- Stomach pain
- Loss of appetite
- Rose-colored spots on chest (in some cases)
- Constipation or diarrhea

Self-Care:
- Complete full antibiotic course as prescribed by doctor
- Drink boiled water only
- Eat soft, easily digestible food
- Rest adequately
- Maintain strict hand hygiene

Prevention:
- Drink only boiled or filtered water
- Avoid raw fruits and vegetables from unknown sources
- Typhoid vaccination available — recommended for endemic areas
- Proper sanitation and hand washing

When to See a Doctor (All Three):
- Any high fever during monsoon season in India
- Fever lasting more than 3 days
- Any warning signs listed above
- Before traveling to endemic areas — get vaccinated and carry preventive medication

Note: These diseases require laboratory confirmation and medical treatment. Educational information only.
