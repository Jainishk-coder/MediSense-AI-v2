DIGESTIVE AND STOMACH PROBLEMS — HEALTH GUIDE

Common Digestive Symptoms:
- Stomach pain (pet me dard)
- Nausea and vomiting (ulti)
- Diarrhea (dast / loose motions)
- Constipation
- Bloating and gas (gas ki problem)
- Acid reflux and heartburn
- Loss of appetite

Gastroenteritis (Stomach Flu / Food Poisoning):
Symptoms:
- Sudden onset diarrhea and vomiting
- Stomach cramps
- Low-grade fever
- Weakness
Causes: Contaminated food or water, viral infection
Self-care: ORS solution, rest, bland diet (BRAT — Banana, Rice, Applesauce, Toast), avoid dairy and spicy food

Acid Reflux (GERD) Symptoms:
- Burning sensation in chest (heartburn)
- Sour taste in mouth
- Difficulty swallowing
- Cough especially at night
Self-care: Avoid spicy/oily food, eat smaller meals, don't lie down after eating, elevate head while sleeping

Constipation:
- Less than 3 bowel movements per week
- Hard, dry stools
- Straining during bowel movement
Self-care: Increase fiber intake (fruits, vegetables, whole grains), drink 8-10 glasses of water, regular exercise, prunes and papaya help naturally

Peptic Ulcer Signs:
- Burning stomach pain between meals or at night
- Pain improves with eating (duodenal ulcer) or worsens (gastric ulcer)
- Nausea, bloating
- Black tarry stools — seek immediate care (sign of bleeding)

Appendicitis Warning (EMERGENCY):
- Pain starting near belly button, moving to lower right abdomen
- Worsening pain with movement
- Fever, nausea, vomiting
- Requires emergency surgery — do not delay

Jaundice Signs:
- Yellowing of eyes and skin (peele pan ka rang)
- Dark urine, pale stools
- Itching
- Requires immediate medical evaluation

Self-Care for Mild Stomach Issues:
- Eat light, home-cooked food
- Avoid street food and unhygienic water
- Drink boiled or filtered water
- ORS for diarrhea — 1 packet in 1 liter clean water
- Probiotics — curd (dahi) helps restore gut bacteria
- Avoid NSAIDs (aspirin, ibuprofen) on empty stomach
- Ginger tea for nausea
- Cumin (jeera) water for bloating

When to See a Doctor:
- Blood in vomit or stool
- Severe abdominal pain
- Diarrhea lasting more than 3 days
- Signs of dehydration — dry mouth, dizziness, no urination
- Persistent vomiting more than 24 hours
- Unexplained weight loss
- Difficulty swallowing

Prevention:
- Wash hands before eating
- Eat freshly cooked food
- Avoid raw/uncooked street food
- Drink safe, clean water
- Chew food properly, eat slowly

Note: Educational information only. Severe abdominal pain needs urgent medical care.
