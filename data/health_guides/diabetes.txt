DIABETES — SYMPTOMS, TYPES AND MANAGEMENT

What is Diabetes?
Diabetes is a chronic condition where the body cannot properly regulate blood sugar (glucose) levels due to insufficient insulin production or insulin resistance.

Types of Diabetes:
- Type 1: Body does not produce insulin (usually diagnosed in childhood)
- Type 2: Body does not use insulin properly (most common, linked to lifestyle)
- Gestational Diabetes: Develops during pregnancy

Common Symptoms (Lakshan):
- Increased thirst (zyada pyas)
- Frequent urination (baar baar peshaab)
- Unexplained weight loss
- Increased hunger
- Fatigue and weakness (kamzori)
- Blurred vision
- Slow-healing wounds
- Tingling in hands and feet
- Recurrent infections

Risk Factors:
- Family history of diabetes
- Obesity and sedentary lifestyle
- Age above 45 years
- High blood pressure
- Unhealthy diet high in sugar and processed food
- Physical inactivity

Blood Sugar Levels (General Reference):
- Normal fasting: 70-100 mg/dL
- Prediabetes fasting: 100-125 mg/dL
- Diabetes fasting: 126 mg/dL or higher

Self-Care and Lifestyle Management:
- Eat balanced meals with controlled carbohydrates
- Include fiber-rich foods — vegetables, whole grains, pulses
- Regular physical exercise — 30 minutes daily walking
- Maintain healthy body weight
- Monitor blood sugar regularly if diagnosed
- Avoid sugary drinks, sweets, and refined flour (maida)
- Eat small, frequent meals
- Manage stress through yoga or meditation
- Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
- Regular foot care — check for cuts and infections

Complications if Untreated:
- Heart disease and stroke
- Kidney damage (nephropathy)
- Eye damage (retinopathy) — blindness risk
- Nerve damage (neuropathy)
- Foot ulcers and amputation risk

When to See a Doctor:
- If you have multiple symptoms listed above
- For regular HbA1c testing if at risk
- Immediately if blood sugar is very high with vomiting or confusion
- For annual eye and kidney screening if diabetic

Prevention (Type 2):
- Maintain healthy weight
- Exercise regularly
- Eat a balanced, low-sugar diet
- Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol
- Regular health checkups after age 35

Note: This is educational information only. Diabetes requires professional medical management.
