HEART HEALTH AND BLOOD PRESSURE — HEALTH GUIDE

What is High Blood Pressure (Hypertension)?
Blood pressure consistently above 140/90 mmHg. Often called the "silent killer" because it may have no obvious symptoms.

Common Symptoms of Heart Problems:
- Chest pain or pressure (seene me dard)
- Shortness of breath (saans lene me taklif)
- Irregular heartbeat
- Dizziness and fainting
- Swelling in legs and feet
- Fatigue during normal activity
- Pain radiating to arm, jaw, or back

Heart Attack Warning Signs (EMERGENCY):
- Severe chest pain lasting more than 5 minutes
- Pain spreading to left arm, jaw, or back
- Cold sweat and nausea
- Shortness of breath
- Feeling of impending doom
- Seek IMMEDIATE emergency medical care — call ambulance

Common Causes of Heart Disease:
- High blood pressure
- High cholesterol
- Smoking and tobacco use
- Diabetes
- Obesity
- Physical inactivity
- Excessive salt intake
- Chronic stress
- Family history

Blood Pressure Categories:
- Normal: Below 120/80 mmHg
- Elevated: 120-129 / below 80
- High Stage 1: 130-139 / 80-89
- High Stage 2: 140/90 or higher
- Crisis: Above 180/120 — seek immediate care

Self-Care and Prevention:
- Reduce salt intake — avoid processed and packaged food
- Eat heart-healthy foods — fruits, vegetables, whole grains, fish
- Exercise 30 minutes daily — walking, swimming, cycling
- Maintain healthy weight
- Quit smoking completely
- Limit alcohol consumption
- Manage stress through meditation and adequate sleep
- Monitor blood pressure regularly at home
- Reduce saturated fats and fried food

Foods Good for Heart:
- Oats, brown rice, whole wheat
- Leafy green vegetables
- Berries and citrus fruits
- Fatty fish (omega-3)
- Nuts and seeds (in moderation)
- Garlic and turmeric

When to See a Doctor:
- Chest pain of any kind
- Blood pressure consistently above 140/90
- Shortness of breath on minimal exertion
- Irregular heartbeat
- Family history of heart disease — get screened annually
- After age 40 — regular cardiac checkup recommended

Note: Chest pain requires immediate medical evaluation. This is educational information only.
