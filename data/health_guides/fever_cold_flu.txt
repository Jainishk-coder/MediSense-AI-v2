FEVER, COLD AND FLU — HEALTH GUIDE

What is Fever?
Fever is a temporary increase in body temperature, usually above 100.4°F (38°C). It is often a sign that the body is fighting an infection.

Common Symptoms of Fever:
- High body temperature (bukhar)
- Headache and body ache
- Chills and sweating
- Weakness and fatigue (kamzori)
- Loss of appetite
- Dehydration

Common Cold Symptoms:
- Runny or blocked nose
- Sneezing
- Sore throat (gale me dard)
- Mild cough (halki khansi)
- Mild fever

Influenza (Flu) Symptoms:
- Sudden high fever
- Severe body ache and headache
- Dry cough
- Extreme fatigue
- Sore throat
- Sometimes nausea

Possible Causes:
- Viral infections (most common — cold, flu, COVID-19)
- Bacterial infections
- Seasonal weather changes
- Weakened immune system

Self-Care and Home Remedies:
- Rest adequately for 2-3 days
- Drink plenty of fluids — water, ORS, coconut water, herbal tea
- Paracetamol may help reduce fever (consult pharmacist for correct dose)
- Use lukewarm sponging for high fever
- Eat light, easy-to-digest food — khichdi, soup, fruits
- Gargle with warm salt water for sore throat
- Steam inhalation for blocked nose
- Avoid cold drinks and oily food
- Isolate if flu-like symptoms to prevent spread

When to See a Doctor:
- Fever above 103°F (39.4°C) or lasting more than 3 days
- Difficulty breathing or chest pain
- Severe headache with neck stiffness
- Persistent vomiting
- Rash with fever
- Confusion or drowsiness
- Fever in infants under 3 months

Prevention:
- Wash hands frequently
- Avoid close contact with sick people
- Get annual flu vaccination
- Maintain good nutrition and sleep
- Stay hydrated

Note: This is educational information only. Always consult a doctor for proper diagnosis and treatment.
